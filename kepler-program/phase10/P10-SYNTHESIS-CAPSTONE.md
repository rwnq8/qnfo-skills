### Title: "The Kepler Program: A Complete Framework for Adelic Quantum Computing"

### Abstract
The Kepler Program establishes that fault-tolerant quantum computing requires departure from purely Archimedean (Boolean) representations. Through Ostrowski's theorem, we prove that any complete quantum error correction scheme must encode logical information in an adelic representation — simultaneously well-defined in both Archimedean and p-adic metrics. We present a complete stack: from mathematical foundations (OFT Theorem) through code constructions (Hensel codec, Bruhat-Tits architecture), physical implementation (trapped-ion proposals, 2-adic biological signal), to philosophical integration (time-as-tree-depth, Autaxys falsification). The program demonstrates that p-adic and ultrametric structures are not optional mathematical curiosities but necessary components of a complete theory of quantum computation and physical law.
