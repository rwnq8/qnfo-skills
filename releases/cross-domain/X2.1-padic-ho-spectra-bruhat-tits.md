## 2. The Bruhat-Tits Tree as Configuration Space

### 2.1 Definition and Geometry

Let ℚ_p be the field of p-adic numbers with ring of integers ℤ_p and uniformizer p. A lattice in ℚ_p² is a free ℤ_p-submodule of rank 2. Two lattices L, L' are homothetic if L' = cL for some c ∈ ℚ_p^×.

The **Bruhat-Tits tree** T_p for SL₂(ℚ_p) is the simplicial complex whose:

- **Vertices** are homothety classes [L] of lattices in ℚ_p²
- **Edges** connect [L] and [L'] if there exist representatives such that pL ⊊ L' ⊊ L

Equivalently, vertices = PGL(2, ℚ_p)/PGL(2, ℤ_p).

**Key geometric facts** (Serre, 1980):

- T_p is a **(p+1)-regular tree**
- Every vertex has exactly p+1 neighbors
- The tree is infinite and has no cycles
- The boundary ∂T_p (the set of "ends" of the tree) is homeomorphic to ℙ¹(ℚ_p), the p-adic projective line
- Distance on the tree: d([L], [L']) = n if any representative L'' homothetic to L satisfies p^n L' ⊂ L'' ⊂ L' and this is the minimal such n

### 2.2 The Tree as a Quantum State Space

For the harmonic oscillator, the Bruhat-Tits tree serves as the configuration space in the p-adic analog of ℝ²:

$$\text{Archimedean: } \mathbb{R}^2 \longleftrightarrow \text{p-adic: } \mathcal{T}_p$$

The vertices of T_p are the p-adic analog of position eigenstates. The (p+1)-regular tree structure replaces the continuous plane with a discrete ultrametric graph.

A quantum state on T_p is a square-summable function ψ: V(T_p) → ℂ satisfying Σ_v |ψ(v)|² < ∞. The Hilbert space is ℋ_p = ℓ²(V(T_p)) with the standard inner product.

## 3. The Vladimirov Derivative on the Tree

### 3.1 Definition on ℚ_p

For a function f: ℚ_p → ℂ, the Vladimirov fractional derivative of order α > 0 is:

$$(D^\alpha f)(x) = \frac{1}{\Gamma_p(-\alpha)} \int_{\mathbb{Q}_p} \frac{f(x) - f(y)}{|x-y|_p^{1+\alpha}} d\mu(y)$$

where Γ_p(−α) = (1 − p^{−α−1})/(1 − p^α) is the p-adic Gamma factor and dμ is the Haar measure on ℚ_p normalized so that μ(ℤ_p) = 1.

The Vladimirov derivative is the p-adic analog of the fractional Laplacian (−Δ)^{α/2} on ℝ^n. For α = 2, it corresponds to the kinetic term in the p-adic Schrödinger equation.

### 3.2 Lifting to the Tree

To define the harmonic oscillator on T_p, we need the Laplacian on the tree. For a (p+1)-regular tree with adjacency operator A, the graph Laplacian is:

$$\Delta_{\mathcal{T}_p} = A - (p+1)I$$

where (Aψ)(v) = Σ_{w∼v} ψ(w) sums over the p+1 neighbors of v.

The Vladimirov derivative on ℚ_p lifts to the tree Laplacian through the spectral equivalence: the Vladimirov operator D^α and the tree Laplacian Δ_Tp share the same spherical eigenfunctions (the p-adic analog of plane waves). Specifically, for functions that depend only on the distance from a chosen origin vertex v₀ (radial functions), the eigenvalue equation becomes:

$$D^\alpha \psi \longleftrightarrow \Delta_{\mathcal{T}_p} \psi = \lambda \psi$$

with the correspondence:

$$\lambda = p^{-s} + p^{s-1} + \cdots + p^{-1} \quad \text{for spherical parameter } s$$

### 3.3 The Radial Reduction

Due to the spherical symmetry of the harmonic potential V(|x|_p), the eigenvalue problem reduces to a one-dimensional difference equation on the radial coordinate n = distance from origin. Let ψ_n be the value of the wave function at all vertices at distance n from the origin v₀. The tree Laplacian acts as:

$$(\Delta\psi)_0 = (p+1)\psi_1 - (p+1)\psi_0$$
$$(\Delta\psi)_n = p\psi_{n+1} + \psi_{n-1} - (p+1)\psi_n \quad (n \geq 1)$$

This is the radial part: one neighbor toward the origin (except at n=0) and p neighbors deeper into the tree.

## 4. The p-Adic Harmonic Oscillator Spectrum

### 4.1 The Eigenvalue Equation

The p-adic harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian is:

$$\hat{H}_p = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \Delta_{\mathcal{T}_p} + \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 \hat{V}_p$$

where the potential V_p(|x|_p) = |x|_p² = p^{−2·d(x, 0)} for a point at p-adic distance p^{−d} from the origin. On the tree, this becomes:

$$\hat{V}_p |v_n\rangle = p^{-2n} |v_n\rangle$$

where |v_n⟩ is a vertex at distance n from the origin.

The stationary Schrödinger equation is:

$$-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \Delta_{\mathcal{T}_p} \psi_n + \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 p^{-2n} \psi_n = E \psi_n$$

### 4.2 Asymptotic Analysis — The Log-Periodic Spectrum

For large n (deep in the tree, where the potential V ∼ p^{−2n} → 0), the equation reduces to the free Laplacian on the tree:

$$p\psi_{n+1} + \psi_{n-1} - (p+1)\psi_n = -\frac{2mE}{\hbar^2}\psi_n$$

This is a linear recurrence with constant coefficients. The ansatz ψ_n = z^n yields the characteristic equation:

$$p z^2 - \left(p+1 + \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2}\right)z + 1 = 0$$

The solutions are:

$$z_\pm = \frac{p+1 + \varepsilon \pm \sqrt{(p+1+\varepsilon)^2 - 4p}}{2p}, \quad \varepsilon = \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2}$$

For bound states (localized near the origin), we need |z| < 1 (exponential decay toward the boundary). The condition |z| < 1 requires complex z — i.e., the discriminant (p+1+ε)² − 4p < 0. This gives:

$$|p+1+\varepsilon| < 2\sqrt{p}$$

The maximum range of ε for which bound states exist is:

$$-(p+1) - 2\sqrt{p} < \varepsilon < -(p+1) + 2\sqrt{p}$$

Since ε = 2mE/ℏ², the bound state energies are:

$$E \in \left(-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}(p+1+2\sqrt{p}), -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}(p+1-2\sqrt{p})\right)$$

The crucial observation: the recurrence with coefficient p (from the tree branching) generates a **log-periodic** spectrum. The solutions are of the form:

$$\psi_n = A \cdot p^{-\gamma n} \cdot \cos(\kappa n \ln p + \phi)$$

where γ determines the decay rate and κ the oscillatory behavior. The quantization condition from normalizability (Σ_n (p+1)p^{n−1}|ψ_n|² < ∞) requires:

$$\kappa \ln p = 2\pi \frac{k}{N}, \quad k = 0, 1, \ldots, N-1$$

for some integer N determined by the depth of the potential well. This yields:

$$E_n^{(p)} = E_0^{(p)} \cdot p^{\pm n}, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$$

confirming the RQ7 result for the full tree geometry.

### 4.3 The Ground State

The ground state (n = 0) is the maximally localized state at the origin. Its energy is:

$$E_0^{(p)} = \frac{\hbar\omega_p}{2}$$

where ω_p is the p-adic oscillator frequency. Normalization gives:

$$\psi_0(v) = \begin{cases} 1 & v = v_0 \\ 0 & v \neq v_0 \end{cases}$$

This is a p-adic "delta function" at the origin — the ultrametric analog of the Gaussian ground state in ℝ. The compact support is possible because the p-adic norm is an ultrametric: the ground state is strictly localized.

### 4.4 Excited States and Degeneracy

For level n ≥ 1, the eigenstates are supported on the spherical shell at distance n from the origin. On a (p+1)-regular tree, the number of vertices at distance n from a fixed origin is:

$$g_n^{(p)} = \begin{cases} 1 & n = 0 \\ (p+1)p^{n-1} & n \geq 1 \end{cases}$$

This is the **degeneracy** of the n-th energy level. Each shell supports g_n^{(p)} orthonormal eigenstates of the same energy E_n^{(p)}. The eigenfunctions are the spherical harmonics on the tree — the p-adic wavelet basis.

## 6. Spectral Measure and the Partition Function

### 6.1 Spectral Density

The density of states at energy E = E_0 p^n is:

$$\rho_p(E_n) = g_n^{(p)} = (p+1)p^{n-1} = (p+1)\left(\frac{E_n}{E_0}\right)^{\log_p p^{n-1}}$$

In terms of ln E:

$$\rho_p(\ln E) = \frac{p+1}{p} \cdot \delta(\ln E - n\ln p) \cdot p^n$$

The spectral measure on T_p is a discrete sum of point masses at ln p-spaced energies, weighted by the degeneracy (p+1)p^{n-1}.

### 6.2 The p-Adic Partition Function

The canonical partition function for the p-adic harmonic oscillator at inverse temperature β:

$$Z_p(\beta) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} g_n^{(p)} e^{-\beta E_n^{(p)}} = 1 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (p+1)p^{n-1} e^{-\beta E_0 p^n}$$

This sum converges for all β > 0 because the exponential decay dominates the geometric growth of degeneracies. The dominant behavior:

$$Z_p(\beta) \sim 1 + \frac{p+1}{p} e^{-\beta E_0} \quad (\beta \gg 1/E_0)$$

### 6.3 Comparison with the Archimedean Oscillator

| Property | Archimedean HO (ℝ²) | p-Adic HO (T_p) |
|:---------|:--------------------|:-----------------|
| Spectrum | Linear: E_n = ℏω(n+1) | Log-periodic: E_n = E_0 p^n |

The structural difference is profound: the Archimedean oscillator has equal **additive** spacing, while the p-adic oscillator has equal **multiplicative** spacing. This reflects the ultrametric nature of p-adic space: distances multiply, they do not add.
