- 976 = 2⁴ × 61 → non-zero p-adic valuations at p = 2 (ord₂ = 4) and p = 61 (ord₆₁ = 1)

**Decision:** Avenue X4 is closed. Tasks X4.2 and X4.3 are preemptively cancelled.

## 1. The Origin of 976/919

### 1.1 Appearance in RQ1

The ratio 976/919 ≈ 1.06202 was noted during the transmon anharmonicity analysis (RQ1) as a curiosity. The transmon scaling law:

$$\alpha_r = A \cdot \left(\frac{E_C}{E_J}\right)^\nu$$

yielded ν = 0.5084 ± 0.017. The ratio ν_measured / ν_predicted = 0.5084 / 0.5 = 1.0168, which is numerically close to — but not equal to — the interval (976/919 − 1) = 0.0620, scaled down by approximately a factor of 3.69. The MWP raised the question: is this a clue to deeper structure, or a coincidence?

### 1.2 The Numerical Structure

## 2. Adelic Factorization

### 2.1 p-Adic Valuations

The only non-trivial p-adic places are:

| Place v | ord_v(976/919) | \|976/919\|_v |
|:--------|:---------------|:--------------|
| v = 2 | +4 | 2⁻⁴ = 1/16 |
| v = 61 | +1 | 61⁻¹ = 1/61 |
| v = 919 | −1 | 919¹ = 919 |
| v = ∞ (Archimedean) | — | 976/919 ≈ 1.062024 |

All other places yield |976/919|_p = 1. The Artin product formula is satisfied:

$$\prod_v \left|\frac{976}{919}\right|_v = \frac{1}{16} \cdot \frac{1}{61} \cdot 919 \cdot \frac{976}{919} = \frac{976}{16 \cdot 61} = \frac{976}{976} = 1$$

### 2.2 Analysis

The factorization structure is simple: only three non-trivial places (2, 61, 919). This is not evidence of special adelic structure — it is the minimum required by the rational factorization of 976 and 919. Any rational number a/b produces exactly this structure: non-trivial p-adic valuations at the prime factors of a and b, plus the Archimedean norm.

The fact that ord₂(976) = 4 matches the number of RG steps on the harmonic ladder (transmon → QCD → EW → GUT → Planck in powers of 2) is mildly suggestive but not statistically significant: one in every 16 integers has ord₂ = 4. Given the vast number of integers that could appear in such an analysis, a single match to the RG step count is not evidence of structure.
