## Purpose

Appendix B situates the Autaxys Generative Cycle within existing philosophical and physical ontologies. For each framework, we identify: (1) the core claim, (2) the structural similarity to Autaxys, (3) the key difference, and (4) whether Autaxys subsumes, extends, or contradicts it.

## B.1 Process Philosophy (Whitehead, 1929)

**Core Claim:** Reality is composed of "actual occasions" — discrete, momentary events — not substances. Process is fundamental; objects are abstractions from process.

**Similarity to Autaxys:** The Generative Cycle IS a Whiteheadian actual occasion. Each cycle step is a discrete event; the URG is the "extensive continuum" in which occasions arise.

**Key Difference:** Whitehead's occasions are atomic and perishing; the Generative Cycle is eternal and self-referential. The URG has no "creativity" (Whitehead's ultimate principle) — it is self-contained.

**Verdict:** Autaxys is a concrete mathematical realization of process philosophy. The Generative Cycle provides the dynamics that Whitehead left unspecified.

## B.2 QBism (Fuchs, Mermin, Schack; 2010–present)

**Core Claim:** Quantum states are not about reality — they are about an agent's beliefs. The wavefunction is a subjective Bayesian probability assignment.

**Similarity to Autaxys:** The observer is central to both. In QBism, the observer's beliefs determine the quantum state. In Autaxys, the observer's cycle phase-locks to the system. Both reject the "view from nowhere."

**Key Difference:** QBism is epistemic (quantum states are beliefs). Autaxys is ontic (quantum states are real cycle configurations). In QBism, there IS no system without an observer. In Autaxys, the system exists independently — the observer is just another cycle.

**Verdict:** Autaxys and QBism agree that the observer is internal but disagree on whether the quantum state is knowledge (QBism) or reality (Autaxys). This is testable: if quantum states exist without observers (e.g., in the early universe before any observer could form), Autaxys is right and QBism is wrong.

## B.3 Relational Quantum Mechanics (Rovelli, 1996)

**Core Claim:** Quantum states are relational — a state is always relative to another system. There is no "absolute state" of a system. "Observer" just means "any physical system relative to which states are defined."

**Similarity to Autaxys:** Both reject absolute states. In RQM, |ψ⟩_S relative to O. In Autaxys, the phase relationship between S and O defines the observed state. The relational structure is the same.

**Key Difference:** RQM leaves the dynamics of relation unspecified. Autaxys specifies it: phase-locking. In RQM, any system can be an observer. In Autaxys, only systems with self-referential cyclic dynamics (re-entrant forms) can be observers — though this may include all sufficiently complex systems.

**Verdict:** Autaxys IS relational quantum mechanics with a specific dynamical mechanism (phase-locking). RQM provides the philosophical framework; Autaxys provides the mathematics.

## B.4 Constructor Theory (Deutsch, 2013)

**Core Claim:** Physics should be formulated in terms of which transformations (tasks) are possible/impossible, not which trajectories occur. The "constructor" is an entity that can perform a task repeatedly and reliably.

**Similarity to Autaxys:** The Generative Cycle IS a constructor — it performs the task "evolve the state by one cycle" repeatedly and reliably. Cycle period τ defines "repeatedly."

**Key Difference:** Constructor Theory focuses on counterfactuals (what's possible?) rather than dynamics (what happens?). Autaxys specifies the dynamics — counterfactuals emerge from cycle constraints. Constructor Theory is more general; Autaxys is more specific.

**Verdict:** Autaxys is a dynamical realization of Constructor Theory's "possible task" space. The Generative Cycle is the universal constructor that generates all physically possible tasks.

## B.5 Digital Physics (Zuse, 1969; Fredkin, 1990; Wolfram, 2002)

**Core Claim:** The universe is a computation. Physical law is the execution of a program. Reality is discrete and informational at the lowest level.

**Similarity to Autaxys:** Both are discrete. The Generative Cycle IS a computational process — step n+1 is computed from step n by applying the generator G. The URG is a computational substrate.

**Key Difference:** Digital physics typically assumes a classical computational model (cellular automata, Turing machines). Autaxys uses a quantum computational model (unitary cycle evolution). Digital physics struggles to explain quantum superposition; Autaxys has it natively (Section 1, Appendix A). Digital physics struggles with the measurement problem; Autaxys resolves it (Measurement Bridge).

**Verdict:** Autaxys is "quantum digital physics" — digital physics done right, with superposition and measurement built into the computational model rather than bolted on.

## B.6 Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, 2004; Oizumi, 2014)

**Core Claim:** Consciousness IS integrated information (Φ). A system is conscious to the extent that it generates more information as a whole than the sum of its parts.

**Similarity to Autaxys:** Both identify self-reference as the key property of observers. In IIT, Φ measures the causal self-reference of a system. In Autaxys, the re-entrant form (QLoF) IS self-reference.

**Key Difference:** IIT is a theory of consciousness, not physics. Autaxys is a theory of physics that happens to explain why observers have self-referential dynamics. IIT's Φ is a scalar; Autaxys's observer is a dynamical cycle with phase.

**Verdict:** Autaxys provides the physical substrate (re-entrant cycles) that could implement the Φ measure in IIT. If Φ > 0 for a system implies the system has re-entrant cycle dynamics, then Autaxys explains WHY Φ-consciousness exists. This is a testable bridge.

## B.7 Panpsychism (Goff, 2019; Strawson, 2006)

**Core Claim:** Consciousness is fundamental and ubiquitous — every physical entity has some form of experience. "Micro-experiences" combine to form macro-consciousness.

**Similarity to Autaxys:** Both place the observer/experiencer at the fundamental level. In panpsychism, experience is in everything. In Autaxys, everything has a Generative Cycle, and observers are cycles with self-referential dynamics.

**Key Difference:** Panpsychism gives experience to everything equally. Autaxys gives observer-status only to systems with re-entrant cycles (self-reference). A rock has a cycle but no self-reference — it is not an observer. A brain has many re-entrant cycles — it IS an observer.

**Verdict:** Autaxys is "differentiated panpsychism" — everything participates in the Generative Cycle (URG), but only systems with re-entrant dynamics qualify as observers. This avoids the "combination problem" (how do micro-experiences combine?) by denying that micro-cycles are experiences — only re-entrant cycles are.
