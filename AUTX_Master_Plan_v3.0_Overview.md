### 1.2 The Quest for Foundational Understanding: Limitations of Current Paradigms

The human endeavor to comprehend the fundamental nature of reality has yielded extraordinary scientific theories and descriptive philosophical insights. Despite these achievements, persistent conceptual chasms and explanatory gaps remain at the very foundations of our knowledge.

Current paradigms often encounter limitations when addressing:

**1.2.1 Ultimate Origins:** The origin of the universe, physical laws, and fundamental constants often rely on axiomatic assumptions or lead to theoretical breakdowns.

**1.2.2 Unification Challenges:** Reconciling general relativity with quantum mechanics, understanding the "dark sector" (dark matter and dark energy), and explaining the hierarchy of forces remain open problems `[established]`.

**1.2.3 Complexity and Emergence:** How novel properties and higher levels of organization (including life and consciousness `[PHILOSOPHY]`) arise from simpler constituents often lacks a truly generative explanation from first principles.

**1.2.4 Ontological Foundations:** Philosophical assumptions underpinning scientific theories are often implicit and unexamined — a phenomenon identified by the Scaffold-Lock Hypothesis `[EXTERNAL-SOURCE: Quni 2025a, DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.21282108]` and the Meta-Pattern of Reification in Physics `[EXTERNAL-SOURCE: Quni 2025f, DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19605446]` as the reification of formalisms into ontologies.

**1.2.5 Limitations of the Autaxys Framework Itself (New in v3.0):** This research framework faces its own foundational challenges. The core claims — the mass-frequency identity $m = \omega$, the Coil Hypothesis, and the broader frequency-centric ontology — remain `[my conjecture]`.
