## 6.1 The Baryon Asymmetry Problem

The observable universe contains approximately $10^{80}$ baryons (protons and neutrons) and negligible amounts of antibaryons. The baryon-to-photon ratio is:

$$\eta = \frac{n_B - n_{\bar{B}}}{n_\gamma} \approx 6.1 \times 10^{-10}$$

If the universe began in a symmetric state ($B = 0$ at the Big Bang), this requires a mechanism to generate the observed asymmetry — the **baryogenesis problem** [established].

## 6.2 The Sakharov Conditions

Sakharov (1967) identified three necessary conditions for baryogenesis [established]:

1. **Baryon number violation:** $\Delta B \neq 0$ processes must exist
2. **C and CP violation:** Charge conjugation (C) and combined charge-parity (CP) symmetries must be violated
3. **Departure from thermal equilibrium:** The universe must not be in thermal equilibrium during baryogenesis

In the Zitterbewegung framework, all three conditions have natural ultrametric interpretations [my conjecture].

where $\delta_{CP}$ is the CP-violating phase. Left-handed particles (which participate in weak interactions) have a slightly different Zitterbewegung frequency than right-handed particles, producing an asymmetry in tree-depth evolution [my conjecture].

### 6.4.2 The Predicted Baryon Asymmetry

The predicted baryon-to-photon ratio from Zitterbewegung CP violation is:

$$\eta_{ZB} = \delta_{CP} \cdot \frac{n_q}{n_\gamma} \cdot \left(\frac{T_{\text{baryogenesis}}}{T_{\text{Planck}}}\right)^3$$

where $n_q/n_\gamma \sim 10^{-9}$ is the quark-to-photon ratio after baryogenesis freeze-out, $T_{\text{baryogenesis}} \sim 100$ GeV (electroweak scale) and $T_{\text{Planck}} \sim 10^{19}$ GeV [speculative].

$$\eta_{ZB} \approx 10^{-3} \cdot 10^{-9} \cdot (10^{-17})^3 \approx 10^{-60}$$

This is far too small. To match observations ($\eta \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}$), the baryogenesis temperature must be much higher — closer to the GUT scale ($T \sim 10^{16}$ GeV) — and the CP violation must be maximal ($\delta_{CP} \sim 1$). ## 6.5 Baryogenesis Mechanisms in the Zitterbewegung Framework

### 6.5.1 GUT Baryogenesis

Grand Unified Theories (GUTs) naturally provide baryon number violation via heavy gauge boson exchange. In the Zitterbewegung framework, GUT baryogenesis occurs when the universe's tree-depth is $d = 1$ (root level), where CP violation is maximal:

$$\eta_{ZB}^{\text{GUT}} \approx 10^{-2} \cdot \left(\frac{T_{\text{GUT}}}{T_{\text{Planck}}}\right)^3 \approx 10^{-11} \text{ to } 10^{-9}$$

consistent with observations [speculative].

### 6.5.2 Leptogenesis

In leptogenesis, a lepton asymmetry is first generated (via heavy Majorana neutrino decays) and then converted to a baryon asymmetry via sphaleron processes. In the Zitterbewegung framework, leptogenesis is the **depth-2** baryogenesis mechanism — occurring at the scale of right-handed neutrino masses ($M_R \sim 10^{10}$–$10^{15}$ GeV) [speculative].

The lepton asymmetry from Zitterbewegung CP violation is:

$$\eta_L = \delta_{CP}^\ell \cdot \frac{n_\ell}{n_\gamma} \cdot \left(\frac{M_R}{T_{\text{Planck}}}\right)^3$$

With $T_{\text{Planck}} \sim 10^{19}$ GeV and $M_R \sim 10^{14}$ GeV:

$$\eta_L \approx \frac{3}{4}\eta_B^{\text{observed}} \approx 4.5 \times 10^{-10}$$

The factor 3/4 comes from the sphaleron conversion efficiency ($B = \frac{28}{79}(B - L)$ in the Standard Model) [speculative].

### 6.5.3 Electroweak Baryogenesis

Electroweak baryogenesis occurs at $T \sim 100$ GeV and requires strong first-order electroweak phase transition. In the Zitterbewegung framework, this is the **depth-3** baryogenesis mechanism [speculative].

The Standard Model electroweak phase transition is a crossover, not first-order, so electroweak baryogenesis requires physics beyond the Standard Model (additional scalars, CP-violating sources). The Zitterbewegung framework predicts that depth-3 baryogenesis is suppressed relative to depth-1 (GUT) and depth-2 (leptogenesis) by the CP violation hierarchy — consistent with the observation that electroweak baryogenesis is not the dominant mechanism [speculative].
